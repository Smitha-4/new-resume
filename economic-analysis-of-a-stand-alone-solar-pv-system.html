Problem Statement¶

A solar PV System having an array of flat-plate collectors is installed in a factory for providing hot water at 70°C The system costs Rs 250000 and is setup with an initial down payment of 20% of the investment. The balance 80% being taken as a soft loan is repaid in equal installments over a 5 year period with an interest of 5%¶

The System supplies 90% of the annual energy required for heating water in the factory, the balance of 10% is supplied by auxiliary sub-system. The conventional fuel used in auxiliary sub-system costs Rs 4700 during the first year of operation. This cost increases at a rate of 5% every year.¶

The annual expenditure required by way of maintenance, manpower, etc is Rs 90000 in the first year, this expense increases at the rate of 5% every year¶

The Tax deduction are permissible only on depreciation, which is allowed at the rate of 25% each year, and the company income tax rate is 30%.¶

Assuming that the market discount rate is 10%, Calculate the net annual costs and the cumulative savings from year to year over a period of 8 years.¶

the cost of an energy system is usually considered in two parts, an initial cost and an annual operating cost, As of now, these system have high initial costs and relatively low annual cost as compared to conventional system. The opposite is true for conventional system.

Generally The net annual cost of system maybe stated as follows

Net Annual cost of energy system = [Yearly costs]-[Yearly benefits]¶

= [Maintenance costs,Local taxes,manpower etc]+[Cost of fuel/electricity]+[Repayment of Loan]- [Tax deducted as available]¶

Cash flows are reduced to compute the equivalent present worth, which refers to discounting. The present worth of money required in some time in future is obtained by calculating how much would have to be invested at a rate called discount rate today, which can come handy in future. The present worth of rupees needed in n years from today is given by 1/(1+d)^n where d is the discount rate. The Sum of present worths is given by

S = FV/(1+d)^n Where FV is the final payment, sometimes it increase constantly by compounding.¶

For the present problem I am assuming that the payment is not compounding.

For the repayment of Loan in equal annual installments,¶

A = dlflC/[1-{1/(1+d)^nl}]¶

where C = initial investment, fl is fraction of loan of the initial investment, dl= interest rate, A is equal annual installments to be maid over nl years.

Tax deduction depends on interest component of A

interest component= [1-((1+dl)^j-1)/(1+dl)^nl -1]dlfl*C But this is not required in the present system.

Tax Saving on depreciation = Cost of System * Company income tax rate/ (1/ tax rate at depreciation)¶

In the present problem the following information is given

  1. Loan years, nl= 5 years
  2. tax rate at depreciation is 25%
  3. Total period n= 8 years
  4. discount rate d= 0.05
  5. loan interest rate dl=0.05
  6. inflation related rates if = 0.05, and im= 0.05
  7. income tax rate= 30%
  8. cost of the system C = 250000
  9. fl, fraction of loan at investment = 80%= soft loan
  10. Cost of auxiliary fuel Cf*E = 4700
